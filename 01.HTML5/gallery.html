Overview & key features

iOS 26 introduces the Liquid Glass design language, expanded Apple Intelligence, accessibility improvements, Always‑On capabilities, and many refinements across system apps such as Camera, Messages, Music, and Wallet.

User interface Apple Intelligence Accessibility Battery Apps

User interface

Liquid Glass: rounded translucent elements with motion-aware refraction, influenced by visionOS and echoing earlier skeuomorphic cues.

Apple Intelligence

On-device models and integrations (including Visual Intelligence, Image Playground, and ChatGPT hooks). Live translation and Genmoji improvements.

Accessibility

Reader Mode, Braille Access, Head Tracking pointer controls, and an audio equalizer with Comfort/Focus modes.

Battery & AOD

Adaptive Power for heavy usage periods, estimated time to full charge on Lock Screen, and wallpaper blur for Always‑On Display.

Highlights

  • Lock Screen widgets at the bottom and dynamic time height based on background objects (with San Francisco font requirement).
  • eSIM transfer directly with Android devices.
  • Quantum‑secure TLS key exchange enabled by default.
  • New Games app, enhanced Camera UX, and Notes call transcription + Markdown export.

App-level changes

goto top

Phone & Calls

Unified optional layout, call screening for unknown numbers, live translations in calls, and per-contact full call history.

Messages

Custom backgrounds, polls, partial message copy, and live translation for sending in another language.

Music

Pin favorites, translation/pronunciation of lyrics, AutoMix (beat-matching), and fullscreen artwork on Lock Screen.

Photos & Camera

3D wallpaper effects, new Camera UI, Lens Cleaning Hints, and HDR screenshots/recordings support.

Accessibility

iOS 26 adds major features aimed at low-vision and blind users, including system Reader Mode and Braille Access (turning the device into a comprehensive braille notetaker), head-tracking pointer controls, and an improved audio equalizer.
